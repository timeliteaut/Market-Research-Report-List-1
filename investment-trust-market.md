<p><p>Investment Trusts, also known as closed-end funds, are collective investment vehicles that pool investors' money to invest in a diversified portfolio of assets. These assets may include stocks, bonds, real estate, and other financial instruments. Unlike open-end funds, investment trusts have a fixed number of shares, which are traded on stock exchanges, allowing investors to buy or sell them at their market value.</p><p>Investment trusts are structured as public companies and managed by professional fund managers. They provide investors with a way to access a diversified portfolio of investments, even with small amounts of money. Investment trusts are known for their potential to generate higher returns compared to other investment options, as they can use borrowing to leverage their investments.</p><p>The investment trust market has been witnessing significant growth in recent years. Factors such as the increasing demand for alternative investments, the rising awareness about investment trusts, and the growing popularity of self-directed investment platforms have fueled this growth. Additionally, the ability of investment trusts to generate consistent income through dividends has attracted income-focused investors.</p><p>Furthermore, the investment trust market has experienced several trends in recent years. One notable trend is the increasing focus on Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) investing. Investment trusts are incorporating ESG factors into their investment strategies to meet the growing demand for sustainable and responsible investing. Another trend is the rise of technology-driven investment trusts, which use algorithms and analytics to make investment decisions.</p><p>Looking ahead, the investment trust market is projected to continue its growth trajectory.
