<p><p>Trauma fixation screws are a type of medical device used to stabilize bone fractures during surgery. These screws come in different types such as stainless steel, titanium, and bioabsorbable. Stainless steel screws are durable and strong, while titanium screws offer high strength with lower risk of corrosion. Bioabsorbable screws are designed to be gradually reabsorbed by the body over time, eliminating the need for removal surgery. Each type of screw has its own advantages and is chosen based on the specific needs of the patient.</p></p>
