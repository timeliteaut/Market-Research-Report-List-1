<p><p>The osmotic laxative market is comprised of various types, including lactulose, sorbitol, magnesium citrate, milk of magnesia, and polyethylene glycol. Lactulose is a synthetic sugar that helps soften stool. Sorbitol is a sugar alcohol that acts as an osmotic laxative by drawing water into the intestines. Magnesium citrate is a mineral-based laxative that works by attracting water into the intestines and increasing bowel movement. Milk of magnesia contains magnesium hydroxide and works similarly to magnesium citrate. Polyethylene glycol is a synthetic laxative that increases water content in the stool, promoting bowel movements.</p></p>
