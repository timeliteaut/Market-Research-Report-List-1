<p><p>Vacuum carburizing furnaces are used in the heat-treating process to enhance the surface hardness of metal components. Single-chamber furnaces consist of a single chamber for carburizing, while double-chamber furnaces have two separate chambers for carburizing and quenching. Multi-chamber furnaces have multiple chambers for performing various processes simultaneously, such as carburizing, quenching, and tempering. Each type of furnace offers different advantages, such as improved productivity and flexibility, depending on the specific requirements of the heat-treating process.</p></p>
