<p><p>Tinted glass is commonly available in two main types: common glass and toughened glass. Common glass is the standard option that is tinted during manufacturing to reduce glare and provide some level of privacy. Toughened glass, on the other hand, undergoes a special hardening process to improve its strength and resistance to breakage. Both types of tinted glass are used in various applications, such as windows, doors, and partitions, to enhance aesthetics and functionality while providing protection from UV rays and harsh sunlight.</p></p>
