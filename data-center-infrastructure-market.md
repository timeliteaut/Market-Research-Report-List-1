<p><p>The Data Center Infrastructure Market is categorized into four tiers based on their reliability, redundancy, and infrastructure. Tier 1 data centers have a basic setup with a single path for power and cooling, offering low uptime. Tier 2 data centers introduce some redundancy but may not have dual power and cooling systems, leading to limited stability. Tier 3 data centers have multiple power and cooling systems with N+1 redundancy, providing higher uptime. Tier 4 data centers offer the highest reliability, with fault-tolerant and redundant components, ensuring the highest availability and uptime levels.</p></p>
