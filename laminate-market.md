<p><p>High Pressure Laminate (HPL) is a durable and versatile surfacing material used in countertops, furniture, and wall panels. It is manufactured by applying high heat and pressure to multiple layers of kraft paper, decorative paper, and melamine resin. HPL is known for its scratch and impact resistance, making it suitable for high-traffic areas. </p><p>Low Pressure Laminate (LPL) is a more cost-effective option that is produced at lower temperatures and pressures. LPL is commonly used in cabinetry, shelving, and furniture where durability is less of a concern.</p></p>
