<p><p>Aviation sealants can be classified into two main types: organic and inorganic. Organic sealants are typically made from polymers, such as polysulfide and polyurethane, which provide flexibility and adhesion to various surfaces. Inorganic sealants, on the other hand, are made from materials like silicone and acrylic, offering resistance to high temperatures and weathering. Both types of sealants play a crucial role in aviation by sealing gaps, cracks, and joints in aircraft structures to prevent moisture penetration and ensure long-term durability and safety.</p></p>
