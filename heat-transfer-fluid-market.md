<p><p>Heat-transfer fluids are used in various industries for transferring heat from one area to another. The two main types of heat-transfer fluids are synthetic organic heat transfer fluids and mineral oils. Synthetic organic heat transfer fluids are manufactured to have specific properties, such as high thermal stability and low volatility, making them ideal for high-temperature applications. Mineral oils, on the other hand, are derived from crude oil and are commonly used for lower-temperature applications due to their cost-effectiveness and availability. Both types of heat-transfer fluids play a crucial role in maintaining efficient heat transfer in industrial processes.</p></p>
