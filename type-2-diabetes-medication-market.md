<p><p>The Type 2 Diabetes Medication Market consists of various types of medications. Biguanides, such as Metformin, lower glucose production in the liver. Sulphonylureas, like Glyburide, stimulate the pancreas to release insulin. Thiazolidinediones, including Pioglitazone, help improve insulin sensitivity in the body. Alpha-glucosidase Inhibitors, like Acarbose, slow down the absorption of carbohydrates. DPP-4 Inhibitors, such as Sitagliptin, increase insulin production and reduce glucose production. GLP-1 Receptor Agonists, like Liraglutide, help regulate blood sugar levels and reduce appetite. SGLT-2 Inhibitors, such as Empagliflozin, lower blood sugar levels by increasing the amount of glucose excreted in urine. Insulins are injectable medications that replace or supplement the body's natural insulin production.</p></p>
