<p><p>Factoring, also known as accounts receivable financing, is a financial service offered by a company known as a factor. Factoring involves the purchase of a business' accounts receivable at a discount in exchange for immediate cash. This helps businesses improve their cash flow and addresses working capital needs. The factor assumes the responsibility of collecting payment from the customers, relieving the business of the task and associated administrative costs.</p><p>The factor typically pays businesses a major portion (around 80-90% depending on the creditworthiness of the customers) of the invoice value upfront and the remaining amount, minus a fee, upon collection from the customers. This allows businesses to access cash without waiting for the payment cycle.</p><p>The Factoring Market has witnessed significant growth in recent years due to various factors. The increasing need for working capital among businesses, especially small and medium enterprises (SMEs), is one of the primary drivers of market growth. The ability to convert accounts receivable into immediate cash aids in business expansion, funding day-to-day operations, and managing unforeseen expenses.</p><p>Furthermore, globalization, international trade, and cross-border transactions have also contributed to the growth of the Factoring Market. The rise in export and import activities has created a demand for factoring services to mitigate the risk associated with international trade, such as fluctuating currencies and late payments.</p><p>Additionally, the rise of financial technology (fintech) has led to the emergence of online factoring platforms, making it easier for businesses to access factoring services. These platforms offer quick and efficient invoice financing with streamlined processes, enhanced transparency, and accessibility.</p><p>Looking ahead, the Factoring Market is expected to continue growing at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 10.9% during the forecast period. The increasing adoption of factoring services by SMEs, the expansion of global trade, and the advancements in fintech are anticipated to be the key factors driving this growth.</p></p>
