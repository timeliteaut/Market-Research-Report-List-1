<p><p>Passenger vehicle steel wheels can be categorized into three main types - casting, forging, and other markets. Casting involves pouring molten steel into a mold to create the wheel shape, while forging involves shaping the steel through heat and pressure. The "other market" category includes variations such as flow forming, which involves spinning a wheel blank while shaping it against a mandrel. Each of these methods has its own advantages and characteristics, influencing factors such as cost, strength, and aesthetics.</p></p>
