<p><p>Motor vehicle leasing refers to a process in which individuals or businesses rent vehicles for a predetermined period, typically two to four years, in exchange for regular monthly payments. This arrangement allows the lessee to use the vehicle without owning it and eliminates the burden of vehicle maintenance and depreciation. At the end of the leasing term, the lessee can choose to either return the vehicle or purchase it at a predetermined residual value.</p><p>The motor vehicle leasing market has been witnessing significant growth, driven by factors such as the rising cost of vehicle ownership, increasing demand for flexible mobility solutions, and the growing popularity of electric vehicles. Additionally, the convenience and flexibility offered by leasing agreements, such as the ability to upgrade to newer models frequently, have also contributed to market growth.</p><p>Moreover, the latest trends in the motor vehicle leasing market include the integration of advanced technologies, such as telematics and connected car services, into lease packages. These technologies provide added value to lessees by enabling features like remote diagnostics, predictive maintenance, and enhanced connectivity options.</p><p>Furthermore, the market is also witnessing a shift towards green leasing, wherein businesses and individuals lease electric or hybrid vehicles to reduce their carbon footprint.
